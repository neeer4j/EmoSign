WHAT IS THIS APP?
-----------------
This app watches your hands through your webcam and 
understands what you're saying in sign language.

Think of it like Google Translate, but instead of 
typing words, you show hand signs and it tells you 
what those signs mean in English!


=====================================================
HOW DOES IT WORK? (THE SIMPLE VERSION)
=====================================================

Imagine the app as a smart friend who:

1. WATCHES your hands through the camera
2. RECOGNIZES what sign you're making
3. TRANSLATES it into words
4. SPEAKS it out loud (optional)

That's it! Let's break it down step by step:


STEP 1: SEEING YOUR HANDS
-------------------------
When you turn on your camera, the app:
- Takes pictures really fast (30 times per second!)
- Finds where your hands are in the picture
- Marks 21 special points on each hand (fingertips, knuckles, palm, etc.)

It's like the app is playing "connect the dots" on your hand.


STEP 2: UNDERSTANDING THE SIGN
------------------------------
Once it knows where your hand points are, it:
- Measures the angles between your fingers
- Checks if fingers are up or down
- Looks at how your hand is positioned
- Compares this to signs it has learned before

Example: If your index finger is up and others are down = Letter "D"


STEP 3: MAKING SURE IT'S CORRECT
--------------------------------
The app doesn't just guess once - it checks many times!
- It accumulates gestures over multiple frames
- Uses temporal buffering with 5-frame stability threshold
- Filters out duplicate gestures automatically
- Waits for 2.5 seconds of inactivity before finalizing

Think of it like quality control: each gesture must be stable!


STEP 4: BUILDING SENTENCES
---------------------------
As you keep signing, the engine:
- Maps gesture sequences to predefined English sentences
- Recognizes 50+ common phrases (greetings, questions, responses)
- Examples:
  * HELLO + YOU + HOW → "Hello, how are you?"
  * YES + THANK_YOU → "Yes, thank you"
  * HELP + ME → "Help me"
- Falls back to fingerspelling for unknown sequences

Like a smart dictionary that learns common patterns!


STEP 5: OUTPUT (TEXT, VOICE, OR BOTH)
-------------------------------------
Once the app understands what you signed:
- Shows the text on screen
- Can speak it out loud
- Saves it to history
- Can export to files
- Can generate step-by-step signing instructions

=====================================================
THREE TRANSLATION MODES
=====================================================

1. INSTANT MODE ⚡
   - Shows each gesture right away
   - Good for: Testing, practicing letters
   - Example: [Shows each letter as you sign]

2. PARTIAL MATCHING 📖
   - Shows hints for partial matches
   - Good for: Learning predefined phrases
   - Example: "HELLO + YOU" → "Possible: Hello, how are you?"

3. SENTENCE MODE 📝 (Default)
   - Builds complete sentences or phrases
   - Auto-translates after 2.5s inactivity
   - Good for: Real conversations
   - Example: HELLO + YOU + HOW → "Hello, how are you?"

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
=====================================================

Q: Does it work without internet?
A: Yes! Everything runs on your computer.

Q: What camera do I need?
A: Any webcam works. Built-in laptop cameras are fine.

Q: How accurate is it?
A: About 85-95% for clear, well-lit signs.

Q: Can I teach it my own signs?
A: Yes! Use the personalized training feature.

Q: Does it work with videos?
A: Yes! You can upload video files too.

Q: Which sign languages are supported?
A: ASL (American), BSL (British), ISL (Indian), 
   LSF (French), DGS (German), Auslan (Australian)


=====================================================
SUMMARY - HOW EVERYTHING CONNECTS
=====================================================

    YOU → CAMERA → HAND DETECTION → ML BRAIN → SEQUENCE BUFFER → SENTENCE MAPPER → OUTPUT
          (eyes)    (find hands)    (think)    (accumulate)      (translate)      (speak/show)

Architecture Flow:

    1. Camera captures hand positions
    2. ML model predicts gesture (with confidence)
    3. Sequence buffer confirms stability over 5 frames
    4. Deduplication removes repeated gestures
    5. Sentence mapper matches sequence to predefined phrase
    6. Falls back to fingerspelling if no match
    7. Display result + speak + save to history

It's like having a translator friend who:
1. Watches your hands
2. Understands sign language patterns
3. Recognizes common phrases (50+ predefined)
4. Spells out unknown words letter-by-letter
5. Tells you (or others) what it means in English

Reliable for constrained vocabulary communication! 🎉
